From the Editor · April 28, 2026
Day 62. The War Powers clock runs out tomorrow, and Trump answered Tehran’s Hormuz offer the way it was structured to be answered: no. The swap doesn’t move on enrichment, which is the one thing the White House keeps saying it wants moved; Tehran wants the blockade lifted first and the nuclear file later, and either side can hold its position because either position keeps things where they are a while longer. The Senate has now blocked six War Powers resolutions on party lines, so the May 1 mark passes without congressional authorization either way, and Democratic counsel is drafting the suit. The Fed held at 3.50–3.75% Wednesday on an 8–4 vote — four dissents, the widest split since 1992. Powell called the inflation read “the noisiest data in a decade,” which is what a chair says when an oil shock has scrambled the labor read. S&P closed 7,135.95. Brent settled near $113, WTI near $109; gas $4.27 nationally, the kind of number that argues with itself. Pam Bondi cut a deal with House Oversight for a May 29 deposition, and GAO opened a review of the DOJ Epstein release the same afternoon — both, finally, on calendars. The Mariners took the Twins 5–3 last night; the Magic put the Pistons out in five. Pour one for Day 62.

IRAN · DAY 62

Pentagon Briefs Trump Today On “Short And Powerful” Strike Plan; War Powers Clock Hits Zero Tomorrow

CENTCOM has prepared a wave of strikes on Iranian infrastructure aimed at breaking the negotiating deadlock, with commanders presenting the option to the president Thursday alongside a separate plan to seize a portion of the Strait of Hormuz to reopen commercial shipping. Trump told Axios the naval blockade is “somewhat more effective than the bombing,” but two officials said he is keeping the strike option live in case Tehran refuses to engage. The briefing arrives one day before the May 1 War Powers deadline, after which the law requires hostilities to wind down absent congressional authorization.
