No. 0 · DRAFT — FORTHCOMING MAY 1, 2026

The deadline arrives anyway

The week in angles. From Four Fingers News.
THE LEDE

It is May 1. Day 63 of the war. The Strait of Hormuz remains shut. The bombers remain at Diego Garcia. The carrier strike groups — three of them, the first time since 2003 — remain on station. The War Powers Resolution clock that has been ticking against the calendar arrives today, and Congress arrives without a vote. The interesting question is no longer whether anything happens by today. It is what the United States has now done, by carrying on past today without a vote, to the constitutional question Congress just declined to answer.

THE COHN READ

Within 48 hours of the WHCD shooting on April 25, the proposed White House ballroom — announced in July 2025, approved by a White House–stacked planning commission on April 2, the East Wing demolished six months earlier — was retrofitted, rhetorically, into a counter-terrorism asset. Trump posted that the attack “would never have happened with the Militarily Top Secret Ballroom currently under construction.” Sen. Lindsey Graham, on Monday: “I’m convinced if there had been a presidential ballroom adjacent to the White House, the guy would have never gotten in.” A $400M funding bill landed by Tuesday.

The frame is textbook Roy Cohn. Attack first — the press is unsafe; the dinner was a target. Deny what the ballroom was ever for — it was always about security; the design just hadn’t gotten there yet. Declare victory — the funding moves; the question of whether a ballroom on the South Lawn could possibly secure a private hotel two miles away never gets asked at floor speed.

The WHCD is hosted by the press. It has been, since 1968, at the Washington Hilton. The president attends as a guest. A ballroom on the South Lawn does not, by any plausible reading, secure that event. The talking point survives only because it is the answer to a different question.

DUDE WATCH · DAY 26

It has been 26 days since the F-15E aircrew known publicly only as DUDE 44 Alpha and DUDE 44 Bravo were extracted from Iran in the largest US Combat Search and Rescue operation in living memory. 155 aircraft committed. 21 in hostile airspace for seven hours in broad daylight. An A-10 lost. An HH-60W damaged. An abandoned Iranian airstrip 23 km north of Shahreza in southern Isfahan, pressed into service as a forward operating base.

In all that time, the Pentagon has not released either airman’s name. Not on a podium, not in a citation, not as a footnote. The Easter parallel was Hegseth’s framing. The silence after is the Pentagon’s.

Today’s question, every Friday, until further notice: Dude, where’s your name?
PAPER vs HARD

The week’s cleanest signal: the Treasury Secretary said the gold is there.

Treasury Sec. Bessent, asked again in March about the Fort Knox audit Trump and Musk floated in February 2025, told reporters all the gold is “present and accounted for.” The audit did not happen. The last time a public audit was performed at Fort Knox was September 1974 — 52 years ago.

Meanwhile, the Banque de France has quietly moved 129 tons of its gold out of the New York Fed since mid-2025 and used the proceeds to repurchase bullion held in Paris. The Bundesbank — 1,236 tons still under Fed custody, the single largest foreign holding in the building — is under renewed pressure from German lawmakers to bring it home. The Russia precedent is in every footnote: $300B in foreign exchange reserves, frozen in a single G7 decision in 2022.

Custody is a story you tell about an asset right up until someone tests it. France is testing it. Germany is being asked, by its own legislators, to test it. The screens will keep printing what they print. The bars are answering a different question.

WILD CARD

On November 28, 2025, at 11:03 PM PT, Mike Borrego closed a 3.5-hour Zeeks-pizza saga — driver Oliver, four cities, a wrong exit on I-405, a second pizza ordered as a hedge that also showed up — with the line “Bogo at least.” Five months and three days later, on April 28, at 8:58 PM PT, Curt Yasutake placed an order for two large pepperonis from Tutta Bella, BOGO. The DoorDash arrived at 9:36 PM, inside the quoted window. No multi-city detour. No second order placed in panic. The driver did not pose for a commemorative photograph.

The Bogo prophecy, twice fulfilled — promotion redeemed, two pies actually delivered — closes a file that took half a year to come together. Filed in full at wildcard.html. Oliver remains a legend.

FROM THE EDITOR

It is May 1. The Strait is shut. The bombers are forward. The deadline arrives. The vote nobody wanted to take goes officially un-taken.

Pour something.
