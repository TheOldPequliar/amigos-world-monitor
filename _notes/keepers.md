# Keepers

*A commonplace book. Lines worth keeping, where I can find them.*

*Rules: one line at a time, minimal context underneath if needed, no essays. Add when something lands. Never delete — old lines sometimes come back different.*

---

> You are, right now, in the middle of the most important parenting work you will ever do, and almost none of it involves talking to them directly about anything.

The sticky-note line. The background matters more than the foreground. Who I am in the room is the lesson.

---

> Coherence is more satisfying than accuracy to the nervous system.

Why worldviews stick. Why good stories feel like truth. The discipline is to notice the hit without being fooled by it.

---

> Pleasure is a bad signal for importance in either direction.

The Protestant-residue permission slip. Some serious things are fun. Some frivolous things are joyless. Feeling is not the test.

---

> The vibe is what determines how the foreground gets encoded into long-term memory.

Kids at 8 and 11 will forget the words. They will remember the atmosphere. Design the atmosphere.

---

> They will forget 95% of the specific things you say to them before they turn 18. They will remember, with near-perfect fidelity, how it felt to be in a room with you.

Corollary to the above. Scarier and simpler.

---

> Staying awake inside your own life is the whole game.

Prosoche. The Stoics knew. Marcus Aurelius held the whole empire and still concluded the only real project was attention. If power didn't let him control events, it's not going to let me either. Stay awake anyway.

---

> The willingness to be caught is what separates a storyteller from a bullshitter.

Great-grandpa Larry's Everest story, caught by his daughter in her sixties. The catch is the test. Feynman loved being wrong.

---

> Pour first. Read second.

The tagline. Also the method.
